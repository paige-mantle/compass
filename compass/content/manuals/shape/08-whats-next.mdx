**Goal:** Pull everything together and decide what to do next.

If you made it to the end, congrats. You've done more product thinking than a lot of people who have had a profitable app for years.

Now we're going to take everything you've worked through and turn it into one useful doc.

This is your **shape doc**: the working source of truth for what you're building, who it's for, why it matters, and what should happen next.

It should be short enough that you'll actually use it and easy to update as things change, which they 100% will. It should also be clear enough that you can paste it into ChatGPT, Claude, Cursor, Lovable, Base44, or whatever you're building with and get a better output back.

## Shape doc format

Your shape doc should follow the same order as the manual:

```txt
1. Product summary
2. Problem
3. Customer
4. Buyer, if different
5. Market
6. Distribution
7. Product shape
8. Demand signal
9. Brand basics
10. Open questions
11. Gaps / research needed
12. Build, pause, or go back recommendation
13. Single next action
```

If you skipped a section, that's fine. Don't invent answers. Leave it blank or write "I don't know yet."

The blank spots are useful because they show you what to research, validate, or revisit before building.

## Review your shape doc

Once you have the first version, don't stop there. Ask your AI tool to review it like a product strategist, not a cheerleader.

<Prompt
  title="Review your shape doc"
  defaultPrompt={`Review this shape doc and be honest.

Please identify:

1. What feels strongest
2. What feels weakest
3. Where the idea is still too broad
4. Where I'm making assumptions without proof
5. Where the customer is unclear
6. Where the product shape is too big for V1
7. Where the positioning or angle is not specific enough
8. Where distribution feels unrealistic
9. What I should validate before building
10. Which section I should go back to first

Then give me:
- a build, pause, or go back recommendation
- the single highest-leverage next action
- 3 follow-up questions I should answer before building`}
/>

## Turn it into a builder prompt

After you've reviewed and tightened the shape doc, turn it into a builder prompt.

<Prompt
  title="Turn it into a builder prompt"
  defaultPrompt={`Using this shape doc, write a builder prompt I can paste into Lovable, Cursor, Claude, Base44, or another build tool.

The prompt should include:

- customer
- problem
- outcome
- product type
- platform or ecosystem
- workflow
- fastest path to value
- V1 scope
- constraints
- non-goals
- brand direction
- tone
- visual direction
- anything the builder should not invent

The goal is to create a simple V1, not a bloated product.`}
/>

## Choose the next action

If the shape doc is strong, your next action might be to build the first version.

If the shape doc has gaps, your next action might be to talk to five users, research competitors, test a landing page, simplify the workflow, or fix the brand direction.

The next step should be specific enough that you can do it this week.

You should have:

- a shape doc
- a list of gaps
