## Who this is for

This manual is for app builders, solo devs, bootstrapped SaaS founders, Shopify app teams, AI builders, and small teams using AI to move faster.

You might have a rough idea, a weekend prototype, a half-built app, or a product that works but is still hard to explain. Either way, this will help you get clearer before you build too much more.

## Why this manual exists

AI made it easier than ever to turn a rough idea into something that looks real. You can open Lovable, Claude, Cursor, Base44, or whatever tool you're using, write a messy prompt, and end up with a working prototype before you've fully thought through the problem.

That speed is powerful, but it's also where a lot of builders get into trouble. A prototype can look finished before the idea is clear. The app has screens, settings, onboarding, maybe even billing, but the basics are still missing: who it's for, what pain it solves, why someone would choose it, and how anyone will find it.

## What we'll do

This manual helps you shape the idea before you build too much. You'll work through the problem, customer, market, distribution, app shape, demand, and basic brand direction.

The goal is not to slow you down. It's to keep your speed from turning into wasted time, burned credits, and an app that is harder to explain than it should be.

## The outcome

By the end, you'll have a **shape doc**: a short working doc you can use as your roadmap, AI context, builder prompt, and source of truth.

It should help you make better decisions before you build and give your AI tools better context when you're ready to move into execution.

<Callout title="Note">

Use AI to organize the work, not invent the strategy. If AI does all the thinking for you, you can't take credit when it works.

</Callout>

## How to use this manual

Keep one working chat open as you go. Use ChatGPT, Claude, or whatever AI tool you like.

As you read, dump your voice notes, rough answers, research, screenshots, links, customer quotes, and half-formed ideas into that chat. Don't try to make it polished while you're still thinking.

At the end of each chapter, you'll use a prompt to organize that thinking into your shape doc.

Best guess for now. We'll validate later.
